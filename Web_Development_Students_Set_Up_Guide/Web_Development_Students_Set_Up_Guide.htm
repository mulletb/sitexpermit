Web development beginning student logging in to everything guide


Forward

When I started this course: I felt overwhelmed with the amount of accounts you needed to be logged into, and, the new student email that was forced upon me. "What is my new email?" "Why can't i use my regular email" "What is my password?" "Why doesn't anything work?!"

In reality: its actually fairly easy, and, it seems rather straightforward and basic once your already started. The problem was: it seemed difficult in the initial "chaos" of getting used to a new situation.

Hopefully: a plain step by step guide of the procedure from the basic beginning might help someone have a less stressful start.


Your Student ID and the clock in


Knowing/acquiring your student ID


Knowing your student ID is the first thing, so: start with that. The course instructor will probably have the proper resources to get your student ID if you don't have it or know it already.

NOTE: if you came here straight from a Lee County high school, your old student ID and student email may work for some things, but, you should/will be issued a new ID number and subsequent email, and, that is the one you will need to be using.


Clocking In

your clock in will simply be your student ID number. The default password should be the last four digits.

Your Student Email

If you are here at your first day of web development, the school already made a separate email account in your name in your absentia.

You log in at https://mail.leeschools.net , and your email is based on your student ID number.
The format uses your ID number as "f12345678@students.leeschools.net" The default password should be your full 8 digit student ID.



NOTE: Most of the sites you will need to log into for this course will need to use your student email to work properly.
